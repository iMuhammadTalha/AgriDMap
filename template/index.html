Project Discussion

Wheat is Pakistan's staple crop, covering 40% of cultivated land and contributing 3% to GDP
Wheat rust is a fungal disease that can cause up to 20% yield reduction, threatening food security
An automated system is needed to localize and predict the severity of rust attacks
The system uses IoT, edge computing, and machine/deep learning, with a camera capturing high-res images of wheat leaves
The Yolo-V7 model detects wheat leaves and a CNN model classifies rust images into healthy, rust-resistant, and rust-susceptible categories
The model is integrated into a mobile app and web portal for easy analysis by agriculturists
The system helps prevent rust spread by identifying attacks and enabling remedial actions.

Outcome

An IoT and AI enabled system (Agri-DMaP) for rust disease detection and predicting its severity level mapping
Mobile application and a web portal to facilitate the agricultural users to visualize the disease data in details and related early warnings
Research publications in well reputed journals and conference publications in prestigious venues.
Workshops and periodic trainings for the developed system

Benefits

Proposed research aims to detect rust disease in wheat crop with enhanced precision.
Rust disease can cause up to 50% loss of yield in wheat, which is the 3rd most produced crop globally.
Using Agri-DMaP to detect rust and take preventive measures can increase the crop yield and reduce the spread of disease.
Saving 25% of crop from rust disease can have a significant impact on overall food market and aligns with the Zero Hunger SDG.
Increased yield can boost the economy of countries like Pakistan, fulfilling the Decent Work and Economy Growth SDG.
Agri-DMaP is a step towards smart agriculture, promoting responsible and optimal consumption of resources and reducing pollution, aligning with the Responsible Consumption and Production SDG.
